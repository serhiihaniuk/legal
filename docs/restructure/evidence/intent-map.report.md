# Summary

This is a static, multi-page educational legal atlas for Ukrainian-speaking professionals working with the legalization of foreigners in Poland, not a decision engine or substitute for legal advice (`AGENTS.md:3-18`, `DESIGN.md:17-26`). Its central journey is `Fakt → Pojęcie prawne → Przepis → Dowód → Czynność i termin → Skutek → Środek zaskarżenia` (`AGENTS.md:50-60`). The product combines a multi-act law library, provision readers, editorial learning modules, a chronological legal map, evidence-document guides, practical case-type guides, and a study plan (`DESIGN.md:28-33`). The intended experience is orientation-first: users identify their place in a foreigner’s case, move into the relevant legal concept or act, connect each condition to evidence, and determine the next procedural action. The visual and interaction system is already tightly specified: warm paper, navy/ink, restrained amber and sage, serif headings, sans-serif UI text, flat surfaces, documentation-style layouts, and keyboard/mobile accessibility (`DESIGN.md:99-121`). Architecture is moving toward canonical typed registries, deep query modules, immutable legal editions, reviewed editorial explanations, and explicit typed references (`docs/architecture/legal-library.md:13-39`; `docs/architecture/document-library.md:26-36`). The principal acknowledged debt is content modeling: repeated shallow templates, divergent copies of legal knowledge, unverified extracted KPA text, source-heavy pages, and weak learning exercises (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:10-30`). `README.md` remains untouched starter-template text and does not describe the actual product (`README.md:1-16`).

# Problems

- **HIGH — Extracted KPA text is acknowledged as unverified.** The audit says it contains defects such as `"organ a dministracji"` and `"w ezwanie"` and “cannot be presented as exact official text” until all 306 entries are validated (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:38-42`). This conflicts with the product’s trust requirement and the source policy requiring exact locators and official verification (`AGENTS.md:81-101`).

- **HIGH — The content has no single canonical knowledge model.** The audit states that fuller case data and shortened map data coexist, document contexts are inferred from incidental mentions, and learning routes are encoded in multiple arrays: `"Немає одного канонічного джерела знань"` (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:53-57`). This contradicts the active design rule that map, case, documents, and KPA should reuse one canonical body of knowledge (`DESIGN.md`, “Content architecture”).

- **HIGH — Universal schemas manufacture apparent depth rather than legal explanation.** The audit reports that map nodes, KPA modules, documents, and case registers are forced through generic structures unsuited to different legal institutions (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:44-51`). `DESIGN.md` now explicitly rejects a mandatory section template and requires topic-specific models, 3–6 meaningful chapters, and explanatory material before checklists.

- **HIGH — README misidentifies the repository as a starter template.** `"This is a template for a new React Router project"` (`README.md:3`) contradicts the mature legal product, legal-risk rules, corpus workflow, and accepted architecture described elsewhere. A restructuring effort cannot safely use README as its entry point.

- **HIGH — Documentation contains conflicting internal-link policies.** The audit decision says internal materials are not linked from educational paragraphs and that prose links are reserved for external official sources (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:6`). `AGENTS.md:172-173` repeats that rule, while `DESIGN.md` defines typed internal legal and evidence-document references embedded directly in sentences. The redesign must resolve whether typed inline citations are a documented exception or whether the older prohibition is obsolete.

- **MEDIUM — Law architecture is declared implemented while retaining extensive migration-state language.** The document says phases 1–6 are active (`docs/architecture/legal-library.md:3-9`) but also identifies compatibility projections and KPA-only artifacts that remain temporary, including `articles.json` (`docs/architecture/legal-library.md:472-486`) and `/guide/kpa` (`docs/architecture/legal-library.md:596-610`). The accepted destination is clear, but completion status is not.

- **MEDIUM — Document-library architecture is accepted but still describes a migration sequence.** Stable IDs and canonical records are non-negotiable, yet legacy title matching remains an adapter “only while legacy map/case data still stores document names” (`docs/architecture/document-library.md:96-101`), followed by a nine-step migration plan (`docs/architecture/document-library.md:147-156`). Architecture redesign should distinguish implemented invariants from pending migration tasks.

- **MEDIUM — Home does not deliver the stated orientation-first entry journey.** The review says it is a route catalog rather than an introductory lesson, duplicates navigation, omits Documents from the learning path, and sends “Start with KPA” to module four (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:78-88`). This contradicts the design principle `"Najpierw orientacja, potem szczegół"` and the requirement that a newcomer understand where to begin.

- **MEDIUM — The study plan is a link catalog, not a measurable curriculum.** It reportedly contains 42 links, almost all to the map, no document or case links, and exercises averaging roughly 17 words without model answers or assessment criteria (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:249-269`). This contradicts the `DESIGN.md` lesson contract requiring a result, full material, document task, answer/rubric, and review.

- **MEDIUM — Document guides overstate their depth.** Ten of eighteen guides lack both acquisition and form/validity content, yet are described as deep guides (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:179-187`). Automatically merged mention-context also introduces unrelated case text and sources, contrary to canonical editorial ownership in `docs/architecture/document-library.md:26-36`.

- **MEDIUM — Case data is structurally rich but rendered poorly.** The audit says document and deadline registers become large vertical `dl` blocks, implementation enum values reach users, and the purported condition/fact/evidence matrix is only `string[]` (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:214-225`). This violates the dense-data contract requiring comparable desktop tables and compact mobile disclosures.

- **LOW — Search language remains stale and contradictory inside older workflow text.** The settled rule is “no search” (`DESIGN.md:33`, `AGENTS.md:172`, `docs/architecture/document-library.md:36`), yet `AGENTS.md` verification still asks to test search and filters (`AGENTS.md`, “Verification before completion”), and the UX audit’s proposed map and document structures still mention search while its opening implementation decision rejects it (`UX-CONTENT-REVIEW.md:6`). These acceptance statements should be normalized.

- **LOW — Product terminology shifts between “case study,” “example case,” and “case guide.”** Current design says a Case is “a full guide to a type of case” and must not substitute fictional personas for legal profiles, while older review passages retain “case study” terminology. This can cause incorrect domain boundaries during restructuring.

# Notables

- The primary learner is not a general consumer: they work with Polish legalization files, speak conversational Polish, and need Ukrainian explanations while Polish legal terms remain intact (`DESIGN.md:24-26`; `AGENTS.md:16-18`).
- The product is intentionally educational and static. There is no runtime API, database, AI interpretation, or background updater in the first architecture; committed local data and PDFs are the operating model (`docs/architecture/legal-library.md:790-800`).
- Legal instruments and evidence documents are deliberately separate domains with different ID and reference types; they must not be collapsed during restructuring (`docs/architecture/document-library.md:22-24`).
- Official PDF/ELI is authoritative. Extracted text is only a locator and learning aid, while reviewed explanation is a separately owned editorial layer (`docs/architecture/legal-library.md:13-26`).
- Edition promotion is a human-approved operation. Fetching, building, AI review, or passing technical checks cannot silently make an edition current (`AGENTS.md:42-46`; `docs/architecture/legal-library.md:28-38`).
- Stable semantic IDs, explicit runtime guards, fail-closed resolution, and edition-aware provenance are strong architectural foundations worth keeping.
- The redesign must preserve old `/guide/kpa` bookmarks through a compatibility adapter while `/law` remains canonical (`docs/architecture/legal-library.md:596-610`).
- The established visual language is warm paper, dark navy/ink, restrained amber and sage; serif headings pair with sans-serif UI text; surfaces are nearly flat with thin rules, small radii, and no content shadows (`DESIGN.md:99-103`).
- Layout is documentation-oriented: compact global header, local index on the left, one naturally scrolling central document, short right-side TOC, and no competing narrative columns. Large selectors belong in a shadcn `Sheet`.
- Cards are exceptional, not the default container. Lists should use rows and separators; comparison-heavy evidence and deadline data should use dense tables/matrices.
- Accessibility constraints are explicit: WCAG 2.2 AA where practical, keyboard-accessible nodes and controls, visible focus, non-color-only meaning, reduced motion, and usable layouts from 360 px (`DESIGN.md:109-121`).
- Sensitive client documents must never be committed. Examples must be anonymized and generalized into facts, legal questions, evidence, risk, and procedural paths (`AGENTS.md:180-188`).
- Open product decisions remain: which anonymized calls to action to add first, whether to track learning progress in `localStorage`, and whether to offer fully bilingual explanations (`DESIGN.md:157-161`).
- `.pi-subagents/` shows substantial recent AI-assisted implementation and editorial work, including incomplete article-review passes. It should be consulted for rationale or failed attempts, but accepted docs and current source files remain authoritative.
